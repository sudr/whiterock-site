Whiterock Conservancy Lodging


Looking for a romantic getaway? Need to rejuvenate? Enjoy sleeping under the stars?



We offer several lodging options. Spend a night in the comfortable, individual rooms at the historic Garst Farmhouse, the home of Roswell and Elizabeth Garst. Or bring your family and friends for a night in one of the cozy cottages.

If you enjoy the outdoors, we offer a rustic 3-season cabin on the banks of the Middle Raccoon River and low-impact campgrounds.

Historic Garst Farmhouse


We are located at 1390 Hwy 141, Coon Rapids, Iowa 50058. Come explore the Roswell and Elizabeth Garst Farmstead Historic District on your own or with helpful staff guidance. Subject to occupancy, guests may visit each of the individual rooms which are full of antiques and historical photographs.


 

Site of the 1959 Khrushchev visit and former home of Roswell & Elizabeth Garst, this inviting 5 bedroom farmhouse is on the National Register of Historic Places. Inside the home are additional rooms which include a collection of artifacts, photos, and library with books and film.

Yard and garden features include outdoor seating and picnic areas, charcoal grill, bonfire ring, shade trees, and flower garden. Fishing ponds and hiking trails are just a few steps away. The main pond has a wheelchair accessible pier and can be reached by vehicle. Buffalo are pastured nearby and are fun and easy to watch.

Stay includes a complimentary continental breakfast in the country kitchen, TV, DVD and wireless internet in all rooms.



Individual Rooms

Garden Terrace

Ornately decorated flora and fauna themed room with private Garden Terrace access on main floor. Includes queen bed and private bath (shower only), reading chair, dresser and TV.

   

Harvest Room

A homey, country feeling room showcasing agricultural history and located on the main floor. Includes queen bed, private bath, reading chair, side stand, dresser and TV. Handicap accessible with a wheelchair access bathroom; small step into the shower with a fold down shower seat.
   

Roswell Suite

A great room for couples! Premiere destination on the second floor decorated in theme of Roswell Garst's life. Includes a king bed, private bath complete with Jacuzzi jet bath tub, reading chairs, dressers and closet for storage, TV and a beautiful view with windows facing three different directions.
   

Elizabeth's Loft

A sweetly, comfortably decorated room on second floor featuring photos of Elizabeth Garst's life and travels. Includes queen bed, reading chair, desk and vanity, closet and TV. Showcases a clever use of built-in furniture. Windows on three sides allows for great views. Shares a spacious bathroom with Toshiama Room.
   

Toshiama Room

A homey, country-themed room with a touch of simple detail as the decor in this room was about the children who were raised in the home. Includes two twin beds, pedestal sink, dresser, reading chairs, and TV. Nicely lit with windows on three sides. Shares a spacious bathroom with Elizabeth's Loft.

Hollyhock and Woodland

 

Hollyhock Cottage
1390 Hwy 141, Coon Rapids, Iowa 50058

Cozy Hollyhock Cottage is set in the beautiful yard with the historic Garst farmhouse next door. The decor reflects the cottage's history, first as the family chicken coop, then hired hand's house and later Elizabeth's garden shed. The renovated cottage offers all amenities, including A/C, a TV and DVD player, and a few family friendly games.

Hollyhock is perfect for a small family or groups of 5-6 people. The bedroom has a queen bed. The living room has twin bunk beds and a queen-sized futon.

The fully-equipped kitchen has a stove, apartment sized refrigerator, dining table, microwave, coffee pot, basic pots and pans and tableware. A picnic table, lawn chairs, charcoal grill, and a bonfire ring are nearby.



 


An excellent fishing pond and the town-loop trail system connect to Hollyhock's yard. An optional self-serve continental breakfast is available next door. Pets are welcome, but must be kept off of the furniture. A dog kennel is available in a nearby shed.




 

Woodland Carriage House
1313 Fig Avenue, Coon Rapids, Iowa 50058

Woodland Carriage House is located in the heart of the Whiterock Valley. The decor has a "lodge" feel, and features the amusing Fluxus Indian Museum (a New Wave art museum with a unique fictional account of the Fluxus Indian tribe).

Woodland is spacious, sleeping up to seven people. The bedroom has a queen and twin bed. The great room houses the kitchen and living room. Two queen beds are also located in this living area. The unit is equipped with A/C, satellite TV, library, and family friendly games.


The fully equipped kitchen includes a vintage but functional wood burning cook stove, an electric stove, full-sized refrigerator, dining table, coffee pot, microwave, basic pots and pans, and tableware.






 



A charcoal grill and picnic area are set up right outside the Carriage House. Located nearby is a bonfire ring, fishing ponds, multi-use trails and the Middle Raccoon River await your adventures.

Pets are welcome! There are outdoor dog kennels available. Dogs are welcome to be inside but only if you are with them and keep them off of the furniture. There is plenty of room in the facility for your own dog kennel.

Oakridge Country Farmhouse


Located at 1564 125th Street, Coon Rapids, Iowa 50058, Oakridge is a very large, private farmhouse deep in the countryside on a ridge overlooking Whiterock Valley. Deer, coyotes, wild turkeys and grazing cattle are frequently spotted from the house.

 



 




Oakridge offers full amenities, including A/C, TV and DVD player, iron and ironing board, family friendly games, and library. The kitchen is fully-equipped and serves up to 30 people. It includes a full-size refrigerator, stove, dining tables, microwave, coffee pot, basic pots and pans, and tableware.

This private yard include a bonfire ring, charcoal grill, and picnic tables. The yard is great for family games such as basketball, croquet, and horseshoes. Fishing ponds are within walking distance, trails and campgrounds are nearby.


Ideal for a family reunion or corporate retreat, this house has an official sleeping capacity of 13. A spacious living room and kitchen allow for serving up to 30 people. Five bedrooms total, the main floor bedroom contains queen and twin beds. Three bedrooms upstairs are usually configured with two twin beds each, though with advance notice, these can be converted to short king beds. The last upstairs bedroom contains two queen beds and a seating area. There are two full bathrooms in the house (1st floor and 2nd floor) and also an upstairs hallway vanity with sink. Many family reunion groups tuck in extra children in the spacious living room and the ample bedrooms.

Pets are welcome! There are two dog kennels are available for use in the garage. Dogs are welcome to be inside but only if you are with them and keep them off of the furniture. There is plenty of room in the facility for your own dog kennel.

Primitive Cabin

 


The 3-season cabin includes a small bedroom (with queen and twin), a living/sitting room (with twin), an upstairs loft (with queen) and a small sitting area. Two queen and two twin foam mattresses on platform beds are provided. YOU MUST BRING YOUR OWN LINENS OR SLEEPING BAGS. Screen windows are covered with shutters. A small table, chairs and a lantern are provided.

In the large yard there is plenty of room to pitch a few tents. A picnic table, bonfire ring, charcoal grill, and clothesline are available. Bring your fishing poles to catch dinner in the backyard pond or river.

805 River Cabin

This remote cabin moved into the valley from 805 Main Street, Coon Rapids, many years ago. It's a rustic retreat, no modern amentities, sitting between a spring-fed pond and the Middle Raccoon River in a beautiful, yet secluded valley teeming with wildlife. Deer, wild turkey, coyote, beaver, and songbirds fill the surrounding woods and nearby clearing. The property includes an old-fashioned outhouse and a shower house with a sun warmed water tank set atop the unit. There is no electricity or running water.


Dogs are welcome at 805 River Cabin. Horses can also be kept at the cabin in an open steel pen (we have two available), on the tie line, or by closing the gate and allowing them to roam within the small fenced in area surrounding the cabin. This site is designated as a strictly hike-in or horseback ride-in site from the Main Campground. It is approximately a 1 mile hike-in site following the river or a few miles by horseback following the main trail. Call ahead to inquire and make your reservation. Absolutely NO ATVs allowed.

Main Campground
1313 Fig Avenue, Coon Rapids, Iowa 50058 (on the east side of the road)

The Main Campground, situated in the heart of Whiterock Valley, is a perfect access point for over 30 miles of dirt trails. The campground sits along a bend of the Middle Raccoon River, amid gorgeous sandstone outcroppings and our resident cliff swallow colony. This particular bluff, from which Whiterock derives its name, is the largest vertical outcrop of Cretaceous Dakota Sandstone in the Midwest.

The campground's 6 sites are very accessible for tents, campers, RVs and horse trailers. The cost per site is $8 per tent per night (with horses, add $2 per rig, per night). Please register at the self check-in kiosk before setting up camp.

Each site is equipped with a picnic table and bonfire ring. The campground area shares a dumpster, port-a-potties, and sun warmed water tank atop a shower house. Limitations: no electric or water hookups are provided, no external generators are allowed, no outside firewood is allowed. Firewood is available for sale in the Main Campground.








 

Several horse tie lines are provided, along with a manure pit and wheelbarrow. Several steel pens are provided (additional fee) and are available on a first-come first-served basis.

When available, the River House yard and dance barn, across the road, offers an additional old fashioned, double-seater outhouse, a year-round hydrant for potable water, and a historic three-story barn in which to play on rainy days.

Several excellent fishing ponds, trailheads, adjacent river with easy access, and a nearby abandoned 1800's log cabin are within hiking distance





 

 

Turkey Ridge Campground

For real privacy consider our Turkey Ridge Campsites. These are located on the edge of a gorgeous woodland and oak savannah. On three separate ridgelines, each site is equipped with a picnic table and bonfire ring. All campsites share a common port-a-potty. Limitations: no electric or water hookups are provided and no outside firewood is allowed. Firewood is available for sale in the campground.

The Main Campground has additional amenities and is a mile hike or three mile drive from Turkey Ridge. Located at the Main Campground is a dumpster, port-a-potties, and sun warmed water tank atop a shower house.

The campsites lie about 1/2 mile off the nearest gravel road, on a sandy flat trail, and are accessible by vehicles. Cost is $8 per tent per night. Please register at the self check-in kiosk in the Main Campground before setting up camp at Turkey Ridge.

Turkey Ridge is adjacent to the Star Field. We have the darkest skies around and this is the best location for star gazing. Pop your tent and watch the skies!

Activities at Whiterock Conservancy


As the recreational services branch of Whiterock Conservancy, our Guest Services activities program offers a family-friendly getaway in the scenic Middle Raccoon River Valley.



Choice of Activities:

Other Local Attractions

 

We are situated on 5,000 acres in the land base called Whiterock. Our land conservancy is dotted with sandstone bluffs carved out by the Middle Raccoon river. We have oak savannas bursting with prairie wildflowers and an abundance of wildlife thriving in its natural habitat.


Whiterock Conservancy has trails which are handicap accessible while others are for the hardy hiker. You're sure to find something to interest everyone in your group.

Inquiries or reservations can be made by contacting the Guest Services at (712) 684-2964 Ext. 112 or guestinfo@whiterockconservancy.org


Free Activities, Year-Round and Seasonal

TRAILS Use of the 33-mile dirt trail system is open to the public. Bring your own equipment for walking, nature photography, biking, hiking, snowshoeing and cross country skiing. Many trails have interpretative signage to increase user awareness. Topics include history of the land, geology, wildlife, and agriculture.

FISHING in of one our ten ponds or in the Middle Raccoon River is free to the public. Several ponds are home to largemouth bass, catfish, crappie, grass carp, and bluegill. The river hosts a healthy population of catfish. A handicap accessible fishing pier is located at the Garst Farm. An Iowa Fishing License is required and can be purchased at Hardware Hank's in Coon Rapids or at the Iowa Department of Natural Resources website. Bass fishing is catch and release unless they are over 18" in which case you may keep these larger specimens.

BIRD WATCHING Anchoring the Raccoon River Savanna Bird Conservation Area, Whiterock Conservancy is visited annually by over 165 bird species. The river corridor is a migratory route for songbirds; wetlands provide habitat for waterfowl; and owls and hawks cruise the woodlands. Whiterock Conservancy is working together with S.O.A.R. (Saving Our Avian Resources) to reintroduce Osprey to the property.

STAR GAZING Find a quiet hill and look up for a great night sky or rent our easy-to-use 8 inch Dobsonian-reflector telescope. Any way you look up, we offer some of the darkest skies in Iowa and the myriad of stars never fail to please. Check our calendar for the annual Star Party held at the end of summer.

TOUR HISTORIC FACILITIES Explore the Roswell and Elizabeth Garst Farm Historic District on your own or with helpful staff guidance. We have brochures available describing various memorabilia and artifacts in the farmhouse. There are interpretive signs thorughout the property describing historical events and current day practices. Subject to occupancy, guests may visit the Garst farmhouse individual rooms, which are full of antiques and historical photographs.

BUFFALO VIEWING Our rejuvenation program for our buffalo herd is a success! Every Spring we expect new calves. The buffalo are maintained on 50 acres of newly-established prairie. You can view the herd by taking the trail (walkers and mountain bikers welcome) starting at the Garst Farm and heading southeast, up the hill. The buffalo pasture will be on your right.

BUR OAK VISITOR CENTER This four-season facility with modern restrooms has a great view of the Raccoon River valley and has aggregate rock trailheads nearby.

PUBLIC ART can be seen at the Riverhouse, the roadside park on Highway 141, and throughout the community of Coon Rapids. The hybrid icons on Hwy. 141 are based on the history and technology of hybridized corn and the Cold War Era visit of Nikita Khruschev.

TOUR THE COLORFUL GARDENS at the Riverhouse, full of colorful cutting flowers.

PICNICKING spots are available throughout the property. Discover the perfect, quiet, and scenic site! You may use our picnic tables or spread a blanket at a site of your choosing.

NATURAL HISTORY exploration. The property has unique geologic formations along with an abundance of mosses, lichens, dragonflies, and damselflies to fascinate all ages of explorers.

MUSHROOM HUNTING for morels and more. Spend a great day in the outdoors looking for your favorite morsel. Do please stay on the Whiterock property and respect our neighbors' privacy.

Activities for a Fee

TRAIL RIDING Use of the 33-mile dirt trail system is open to the public for horseback riding (you bring the horse!) and use of Whiterock Conservancy's John Deere Gator™. Trail maps are available at the farm office and main campground or download here.

RIDE A JOHN DEERE GATOR™ An excellent way to go fishing, explore the uplands along the river or find a private picnic spot.

HAYRACK RIDES Hop on a tractor pulled hay-rack and head out on a riverside jaunt over rolling hills and through our prairie and savanna restoration projects.

RENT AN ADULT SIZED TRAIL BIKE and explore the paved and crushed rock trails on our property and adjacent Raccoon Valley trail system.

PADDLING The scenic 8-mile Middle Raccoon River Water Trail starts at Riverside Park in Coon Rapids and ends at our main campground. This scenic float takes approximately two to five hours (depending on put in location and water flow). Paddling is dependent on the river's water level. Paddlers can check with the staff to make sure the water is navigable. Ten aluminum canoes and eight kayaks are available for rent. Canoes hold 2-3 passengers and kayaks hold 1. Shuttle service, life vests and paddles are provided along with rental.

RENT FISHING EQUIPMENT Basic equipment sets are available for rent. Handicap accessible fishing pier is located at the historic Garst Farm. An Iowa fishing license is required.

HUNTING Reservations are required. Paid hunting opportunities at Whiterock Conservancy include bow hunting for deer and shotgun turkey hunting (non toxic shot only). Our guided hunting services include scouting, placement, and tracking, when applicable, as well as assistance in the field-dressing of harvested animals. For out-of-state residents, this land is a phenomenal resource for sportsmen and is worth the effort to obtain a hunting license. The Iowa Department of Natural Resources provides hunting licenses for both in-state and out-of-state hunters

GUIDED TOURS offer entertainment and fun learning opportunities. Khrushchev in Iowa is a fascinating story about mid 20th-century agriculture and an unlikely friendship in the middle of the Cold War, often told by a Garst granddaughter. Other tour options include a Agricultural Tours, Habitats of Whiterock, and Raptor Workshops (Birds of Prey). Custom programs can be developed to suit your interests.

GUIDED NATURE HIKE Explore Iowa's outdoors on a guided hiking tour tailored to your group's interests, size and abilities. Discover wetland seeps along the river valley, check out prairie and savanna restorations, go creek-walking during the heat of summer or scout the land for signs of wildlife by looking for tracks or hooting for owls.

FARM HISTORY / KHRUSHCHEV TALK The unique history of the Garst Family and Khrushchev visit told by Liz Garst is a fascinating lesson of a historical event that put a thaw in the Cold War. Hear about the development of Roswell Garst's career, his developments in modern Midwestern agriculture, and efforts to sell seed corn to promote world peace. The history talk is complimented by the many amazing photographs by Joe Munroe featured at the Roswell and Elizabeth Garst Farmstead Historic District.

ALTERNATIVE AGRICULTURE TOUR Meet with Darwin Pierce, Whiterock's Farm Manager for an informative talk on agricultural history in the region and around the world. Tours can also be scheduled to visit Wiggles LLC, a local vermiculture operation (worm farm).

RAPTOR PRESENTATION Learn about the native Iowa raptors (birds of prey) through live demonstrations of hunting hawks and educational birds. This program is presented by our local avian biologist and rehabilitator, Kay Neumann, founder and director of S.O.A.R. (Saving Our Avian Resources).

See our Price List for further information (Activities are in the lower section).
